## 1. Mission

Provide Eunoia creators with a real-time, AI-augmented view of every revenue stream generated by their content — across platforms, deal types, and time horizons — so that financial decisions are driven by data rather than estimation.

---

## 2. Business Goal

Video creators typically manage revenue from five or more sources simultaneously (ad revenue, sponsorships, memberships, merchandise, licensing) with no unified view. Reconciling these streams requires manual spreadsheet work that is error-prone, delayed by 30–60 days due to platform reporting lags, and incapable of forecasting.

Eunoia Revenue Intelligence eliminates this gap by ingesting all revenue signals, normalising them into a unified ledger, and applying AI forecasting to surface projections, anomalies, and optimisation opportunities in a single interface. The business goal is to reduce the time a creator spends on financial reconciliation from an average of 8 hours per month to under 30 minutes, while increasing revenue per video by at least 15% through AI-guided monetisation decisions.

---

## 3. Scope

- Real-time ingestion of monetisation data from YouTube (AdSense, memberships, Super Thanks), and future expansion to other platforms via EES-003 publishing connectors.
- Brand deal pipeline: proposal tracking, valuation, negotiation history, payment milestones.
- Membership and recurring revenue tracking across platforms.
- AI revenue forecasting at 7-day, 30-day, and 90-day horizons.
- Anomaly detection for unexpected revenue drops or spikes.
- Per-video revenue attribution with cost-of-production overlay.
- Revenue dashboard with breakdown by stream, period, and content type.
- Exportable financial summaries in CSV and JSON formats.
- Webhook-driven event publication on all revenue state changes.

---

## 4. Out of Scope

- Tax filing, accounting compliance, or general ledger integration. Revenue Intelligence is a planning and analytics tool, not a bookkeeping system.
- Payment processing or disbursement. Eunoia does not move money.
- Real-time YouTube AdSense data — YouTube's API enforces a 24–72 hour reporting delay; the system reflects this latency transparently.
- Merchandise fulfilment or inventory management.
- Revenue sharing with collaborators or co-producers (handled by EES-007 Agency OS).

## 9. Business Rules

**BR-001 — Platform Lag Disclosure:** All ingested platform revenue figures must display the data cutoff timestamp. The UI must never present delayed data as current without surfacing the lag to the creator.

**BR-002 — Currency Normalisation:** All monetary values are stored internally in USD with four decimal places. Converted values from non-USD platforms must store the original currency, original amount, exchange rate, and conversion timestamp alongside the USD equivalent.

**BR-003 — Brand Deal Immutability:** Once a brand deal payment milestone is marked as received, its monetary value becomes immutable. Corrections require a credit/debit adjustment entry, preserving the original record.

**BR-004 — Negative Revenue Handling:** YouTube clawbacks and refunds are represented as negative entries in the ledger and must propagate to forecasting models as corrective signals.

**BR-005 — Forecast Invalidation:** If a creator's channel loses monetisation status, all active forecasts are invalidated immediately and the creator is notified. Forecasting does not resume until monetisation is restored and at least 14 days of post-restoration data have been collected.

**BR-006 — Minimum Data Threshold for AI Forecasting:** The forecasting model requires a minimum of 90 days of historical revenue data before generating forecasts. Below this threshold, the system surfaces the raw trend only and clearly labels it as insufficient for AI-driven projection.

**BR-007 — Brand Deal Valuation Floor:** AI-generated brand deal valuations apply a floor equal to the creator's trailing 30-day CPM multiplied by the estimated audience reach of the deliverable. No AI valuation may be returned below this floor.

**BR-008 — Anomaly Definition:** A revenue anomaly is defined as a daily figure that deviates more than 2.5 standard deviations from the trailing 30-day rolling mean. Both upward and downward anomalies are flagged.

## 10. AI Decision Flow

### 10.1 Revenue Forecasting

**Intent:** Predict revenue for 7, 30, and 90-day horizons by stream type.

**Inputs:**
- Historical daily revenue ledger (per stream)
- Upcoming publish schedule from EES-003
- Seasonal adjustment signals (holidays, platform events)
- Trailing 90-day content performance metrics from EES-004
- Creator's historical revenue volatility coefficient

**Reasoning Chain:**
1. Decompose historical revenue into trend, seasonality, and residual components.
2. Score upcoming scheduled content against historical performance patterns for similar content types to generate expected ad impression volumes.
3. Apply stream-specific lag corrections (e.g., YouTube reports month-end CPM premiums).
4. Generate point estimates and confidence intervals for each horizon.
5. Blend stream-level forecasts into a total revenue projection.

**Output Contract:**
```json
{
  "forecastId": "string (uuid)",
  "generatedAt": "ISO 8601 timestamp",
  "horizons": {
    "7day": { "estimate": "number", "low": "number", "high": "number", "confidenceLevel": 0.80 },
    "30day": { "estimate": "number", "low": "number", "high": "number", "confidenceLevel": 0.70 },
    "90day": { "estimate": "number", "low": "number", "high": "number", "confidenceLevel": 0.55 }
  },
  "byStream": {
    "adRevenue": { "7day": "number", "30day": "number", "90day": "number" },
    "memberships": { "7day": "number", "30day": "number", "90day": "number" },
    "brandDeals": { "7day": "number", "30day": "number", "90day": "number" }
  },
  "dataQuality": "sufficient | marginal | insufficient",
  "forecastVersion": "string"
}
```

**Failure Modes:** If insufficient history exists (< 90 days), return `dataQuality: "insufficient"` and omit confidence intervals. If the platform API has not refreshed within 24 hours, append a `dataLagWarning` flag.

---

### 10.2 Brand Deal Valuation

**Intent:** Estimate the fair market value of a brand deal based on channel metrics and deal parameters.

**Inputs:**
- Channel subscribers, average views per video (trailing 90 days)
- Engagement rate (comments + likes / views)
- Audience demographic signals (if available via platform API)
- Deal parameters: deliverable type (dedicated video, integration, post), exclusivity, usage rights duration
- Industry vertical of the brand

**Reasoning Chain:**
1. Compute baseline CPM from trailing ad revenue and impression data.
2. Apply deliverable type multiplier (dedicated = 1.0x, integration = 0.45x, community post = 0.15x).
3. Apply exclusivity premium (30-day exclusivity = +20%, 90-day = +40%).
4. Apply usage rights multiplier (extended rights = +25% per additional year).
5. Apply industry vertical adjustment based on vertical-specific CPM data.
6. Return estimate with floor enforcement per BR-007.

**Output Contract:**
```json
{
  "dealId": "string",
  "estimatedValue": "number (USD)",
  "floor": "number (USD)",
  "breakdown": {
    "baselineCPM": "number",
    "deliverableMultiplier": "number",
    "exclusivityPremium": "number",
    "usageRightsMultiplier": "number",
    "industryAdjustment": "number"
  },
  "confidence": "high | medium | low",
  "dataAsOf": "ISO 8601 timestamp"
}
```

---

### 10.3 Anomaly Detection

**Intent:** Flag revenue deviations that require creator attention.

**Inputs:** Daily revenue totals per stream for the trailing 90 days.

**Reasoning Chain:**
1. Compute 30-day rolling mean and standard deviation per stream.
2. Compare today's value against the 2.5σ threshold.
3. If threshold breached, classify as spike or drop.
4. Check for known explanatory signals (holiday, viral video, newly published video).
5. If no explanatory signal found, emit anomaly event with severity rating.

**Output Contract:**
```json
{
  "anomalyId": "string (uuid)",
  "stream": "adRevenue | memberships | brandDeals",
  "detectedAt": "ISO 8601 timestamp",
  "direction": "spike | drop",
  "severity": "low | medium | high",
  "actualValue": "number",
  "expectedValue": "number",
  "deviationSigma": "number",
  "explanation": "string | null",
  "requiresCreatorAction": "boolean"
}
```

## 13. APIs

### GET /api/v1/channels/:channelId/revenue/summary

Returns the unified revenue summary for a channel.

**Query Parameters:**
- `start` (ISO 8601 date, required)
- `end` (ISO 8601 date, required)
- `streams` (comma-separated RevenueStream values, optional — defaults to all)

**Response 200:**
```json
{
  "channelId": "string",
  "period": { "start": "string", "end": "string" },
  "total": "number",
  "byStream": {
    "adRevenue": "number",
    "memberships": "number",
    "brandDeals": "number",
    "superThanks": "number"
  },
  "recordCount": "number",
  "dataLag": { "adRevenue": "ISO 8601 timestamp | null" }
}
```

---

### GET /api/v1/channels/:channelId/revenue/forecast

Returns the current active forecast.

**Response 200:** `RevenueForecast` object as defined in Section 11.

---

### POST /api/v1/channels/:channelId/deals

Creates a new brand deal record.

**Request Body:** `BrandDeal` (excluding `id`, `createdAt`, `updatedAt`, `estimatedValue` — these are server-generated).

**Response 201:** Full `BrandDeal` object including AI-generated `estimatedValue`.

---

### PATCH /api/v1/channels/:channelId/deals/:dealId

Updates deal status or metadata. Milestones may not be edited via this endpoint.

---

### POST /api/v1/channels/:channelId/deals/:dealId/milestones/:milestoneId/mark-paid

Marks a payment milestone as received. Response includes the updated immutable record and triggers `revenue.deal.milestone_paid` event.

---

### GET /api/v1/channels/:channelId/revenue/records

Returns paginated revenue records.

**Query Parameters:** `start`, `end`, `stream`, `source`, `page`, `limit` (max 500).

---

### POST /api/v1/channels/:channelId/revenue/export

Generates an export file. Returns a signed download URL valid for 10 minutes.

**Request Body:** `{ format: "csv" | "json", start: string, end: string }`

## 15. Security

**Authentication:** All API endpoints require a valid Supabase JWT. Channels are scoped to authenticated users; cross-channel access is prohibited.

**Revenue Data Sensitivity:** Revenue figures and brand deal terms are classified as sensitive financial data. They are never included in log output, error messages, or analytics payloads. Database columns storing monetary values use row-level security (RLS) policies in Supabase enforcing single-owner access.

**Brand Deal Confidentiality:** Deal terms (agreed value, brand name) are accessible only to the channel owner. No deal data is shared with other system actors, plugins, or AI models without explicit creator consent.

**API Rate Limits:** Platform connector polling is rate-limited to respect each platform's API quota. Connectors implement exponential backoff with jitter. Quota exhaustion events are logged and surfaced to the creator.

**Export Security:** Exported files are generated server-side, stored in ephemeral storage, and accessed via signed URLs with a 10-minute TTL. Exports are not cached.

**Audit Log:** All mutations to the revenue ledger are appended to an immutable audit log including actor identity, timestamp, IP address, and change delta.

| AI valuation used as negotiation ceiling by brands | Low | Medium | Valuation is creator-private; never surface to brand-facing interfaces |

## 18. Acceptance Criteria

**AC-001 — Revenue Ingestion:** Given that a creator's YouTube channel is connected and the platform API is available, when the ingestion connector runs, then new revenue records appear in the ledger within 4 hours of platform availability, with correct currency normalisation and data lag timestamps.

**AC-002 — Forecast Generation:** Given a channel with at least 90 days of revenue history, when a forecast is requested, then the system returns estimates for 7, 30, and 90-day horizons with confidence intervals and correctly labelled data quality.

**AC-003 — Insufficient Data Handling:** Given a channel with fewer than 90 days of revenue history, when a forecast is requested, then the system returns `dataQuality: "insufficient"` and does not return confidence intervals.

**AC-004 — Brand Deal Valuation:** Given a new brand deal record with valid deliverable type and channel metrics available, when the deal is created, then the estimated value is returned within the creation response, is above the floor defined in BR-007, and includes a full breakdown.

**AC-005 — Anomaly Detection:** Given a daily revenue figure that exceeds 2.5 standard deviations from the 30-day rolling mean, when the anomaly detector runs, then a `revenue.anomaly.detected` event is published within one detection cycle.

**AC-006 — Milestone Immutability:** Given a deal milestone that has been marked as paid, when an attempt is made to modify its amount, then the system returns an error and the original record is unchanged.

**AC-007 — Export:** Given a valid date range request, when an export is triggered, then a signed download URL is returned within 30 seconds and the file correctly reflects all ledger entries for the period.

**AC-008 — Cross-Channel Isolation:** Given creator A's JWT, when an API request targets creator B's channel, then the system returns 403 Forbidden and logs the access attempt.
